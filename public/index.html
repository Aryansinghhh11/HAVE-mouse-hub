Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about choosing the right mouse

Most competitive gamers use 400–800 DPI with a low in-game sensitivity. Higher DPI (1600+) can be useful for productivity or high-resolution displays. The key is finding a combination of DPI and sensitivity that gives you consistent aim.

Modern wireless mice (like the Logitech Lightspeed or Razer HyperSpeed) have virtually zero latency difference compared to wired. The convenience of wireless is now achievable without any performance compromise.

Weight is a personal preference. Lighter mice (under 60g) allow faster flick movements and reduce fatigue during long sessions. Heavier mice offer more control and stability. Try both to see what feels natural to you.

Palm grip: Best for ergonomic mice (DeathAdder, EC2). Claw grip: Works well with medium-sized mice. Fingertip grip: Ideal for small, lightweight mice (Viper Mini, UltralightX). Match your grip to the mouse shape for maximum comfort.

Use a can of compressed air to blow dust from the sensor lens. For stubborn debris, gently use a cotton swab with isopropyl alcohol. Clean your mousepad regularly too — a dirty pad affects tracking more than a dirty sensor.
